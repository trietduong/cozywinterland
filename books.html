Enjoy reading these books under cozy blankets

1. Murder on the Orient Express

I have always found it fitting to curl up in bed and enjoy a well-written thriller book on cold winter nights. Murder on the Orient Express is a classic whodunnit, the murder takes place on a train stuck in a snowdrift, making this book perfect for the snowy season.

2. The Golden Compass

It is easy to feel bored at home when it is too cold to go outside. Why don’t you hop along Lyra’s magical adventure in this children's fantasy novel?

3. The secret history

One of the most amazing books that I have ever read, the novel is set in a small college town in New England. Greek, literature, philosophy, strange friendships, murders, and long winter days are the theme of this book.

4. The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy

If “The Secret History” is too dark for your liking, “The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy” with its weird, funny characters and exciting journeys around the universe may be a better choice.

5. The Call of the Wild

Winter may be fun but is also harsh and maybe deadly. This adventure novel takes place in the real world: the harsh and unforgiving land of frozen Alaska. If you love dogs and enjoy the wilderness, this is the book for you.

6. A Christmas Carol

A classic to read (and reread) during the holiday season. It is easy to be swept away in the business of everyday life. The slow winter nights and the end of the year are a chance for us to reflect and cherish what is truly important in our lives.

7. Owls of the Eastern Ice: A Quest to Find and Save the World's Largest Owl

The only nonfiction book on this list. This book documents a scientist's arduous field trips and research in his quest to track the elusive Blakiston's Fish Owl in eastern Russian forests during the cold and hard winter.
